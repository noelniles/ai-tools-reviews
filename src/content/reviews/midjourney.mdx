Let's address the elephant in the room: Midjourney still runs through Discord in 2026. Yes, really.

No website. No native app. You have to use Discord—a gaming chat platform—to generate images. It's weird, it's clunky, and it drives a lot of people crazy.

And yet... Midjourney produces the most beautiful AI images I've ever seen.

I've generated over 3,000 images with Midjourney since v4. I've tried DALL-E 3, Stable Diffusion, Firefly, Leonardo—all the alternatives. Nothing comes close to Midjourney's aesthetic quality. Nothing.

Is it worth dealing with Discord? That depends on what you need. Let me break it down.

## My Testing Setup

I've used Midjourney on and off since September 2022 (v3 era). Tested v4, v5, v5.2, and now v6. Generated images for:
- **Blog headers** (this is my primary use case)
- **Social media posts** (Instagram, LinkedIn)
- **Product mockups** and concept designs
- **Personal art projects** (just for fun)
- **Client presentations** (as a freelance marketer)

Total spent: ~$480 over 18 months. Generated about 3,200 images.

Compared directly against DALL-E 3 (via ChatGPT Plus) and Stable Diffusion (ComfyUI) doing the same prompts. Kept screenshots of everything.

## What Makes Midjourney Special

**The aesthetic is unmatched**

I don't know how else to say this: Midjourney images just *look better*. Better composition. Better lighting. Better color grading. It's like comparing iPhone photos to a mirrorless camera—the technical specs might be similar, but the output quality is noticeably different.

Example: I generated "a cozy coffee shop at sunset" in all three tools. DALL-E 3 gave me a technically accurate but boring image. Stable Diffusion gave me something that looked AI-generated. Midjourney gave me something that looked like it came from a professional photographer's portfolio.

**It handles artistic styles incredibly well**

Want anime? Watercolor? Oil painting? Vintage photography? Midjourney nails them all. DALL-E 3 can do styles too, but they often feel... safe. Sanitized. Midjourney's outputs have personality.

**The community is actually useful**

Because everyone generates images in shared Discord channels (yes, this is weird), you can see what prompts other people use. I've learned so much just by watching the feed and copying techniques.

There's also `/describe` command where you upload an image and Midjourney tells you prompts that would generate something similar. This is *insanely* useful for learning.

## The Discord Problem (Let's Be Real)

This is where I lose people.

**You HAVE to use Discord.** There's no web interface. No mobile app (well, you can use Discord mobile, but it's painful). You join a Discord server, type `/imagine` followed by your prompt, and wait.

Your images generate in public channels where hundreds of other people are also generating images. It's chaos. Images fly by at lightning speed. If you look away for 10 seconds, your image is buried under 50 other generations.

You can pay extra ($20/month more) for "private mode" where your generations are hidden. But they're still in Discord.

**Why hasn't Midjourney built a proper interface?**

Great question. I've asked this myself about 100 times. The team seems committed to Discord for some reason. There are rumors of a web interface "coming soon" but I've heard that for 2 years now.

**Is it a dealbreaker?**

For some people, yes. I have designer friends who refuse to touch Midjourney purely because of Discord. They use DALL-E 3 instead and accept the lower quality.

For me? I've gotten used to it. Is it annoying? Absolutely. Do I wish there was a clean web interface? Every single day. But the image quality is worth the friction.

## Pricing: More Expensive Than You Think

Here's what it actually costs:

- **Basic:** $10/month - ~200 generations (about 6-7 per day)
- **Standard:** $30/month - ~900 generations (my tier)
- **Pro:** $60/month - ~1800 generations + stealth mode
- **Mega:** $120/month - ~3600 generations + stealth mode

**My take:** The Basic plan is basically useless unless you're extremely casual. 200 generations sounds like a lot until you realize you'll generate 4-5 variations of each image trying to get it right.

I'm on Standard ($30/month) and I hit my limit about twice a month when I have client work. That's when I either wait or upgrade temporarily.

The $20 upcharge for stealth mode (Pro plan) is... controversial. You're literally just paying to hide your generations from public view. Some people think it's worth it for client work. I think it's overpriced.

**Compared to alternatives:**
- DALL-E 3 via ChatGPT Plus: $20/month (unlimited with rate limits)
- Stable Diffusion: Free (if you run it locally)
- Leonardo.ai: $12/month for similar features

Midjourney is objectively more expensive. The question is whether the quality difference is worth it.

## My Honest Verdict

I've tried to quit Midjourney three times. I always come back.

**Why I keep trying to quit:** Discord is annoying, $30/month feels expensive, and DALL-E 3 is getting better every month.

**Why I keep coming back:** The images are just *better*. When I post Midjourney images on social media, people assume they're professional photos. When I post DALL-E images, they look... fine. But there's a difference.

If you're a casual user who generates 5-10 images a month, use DALL-E 3 through ChatGPT Plus. The $20/month gets you unlimited generations (with rate limits) plus all of ChatGPT.

But if you're a designer, marketer, content creator, or anyone who needs consistently beautiful images and uses them regularly? Midjourney is worth the premium.

Just prepare yourself for the Discord experience.

For anyone serious about AI art—whether for professional work, marketing materials, or creative projects—Midjourney is worth the investment.
